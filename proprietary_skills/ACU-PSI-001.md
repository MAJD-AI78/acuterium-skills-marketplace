### Tone Classification System

The tone score is the single most operationally significant component of η_i. Institutional decision-makers respond to tone before evaluating content.

| Tone Type | Score | Description | Risk | PSI Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Authoritative-Neutral | 1.00 | Rule-of-law framing; zero emotional language; treats correction as institutional self-protection | None | APPROVED |
| Professional-Assertive | 0.85 | Firm, clear, factual positioning with respectful confidence | Low | APPROVED |
| Diplomatic-Firm | 0.80 | Deferential framing with clear underlying position | Low | APPROVED |
| Collegial-Professional | 0.75 | Collaborative tone; risks appearing uncertain | Moderate | CONDITIONAL |
| Defensive | 0.50 | Reactive framing; implies the institution was wrong to act; triggers authority bias | Moderate-High | REVISE |
| Adversarial | 0.30 | Accusatory, confrontational, or implicitly challenging panel authority | High — activates PJS | REJECT |
| Emotional | 0.20 | Appeals to sympathy, fairness grievance, or personal impact | Critical | REJECT |

**Tone Detection Triggers:**

```
DEFENSIVE TONE INDICATORS (score → 0.50):
- Phrases beginning with "we disagree with..."
- Counterfactual framing: "the panel failed to consider..."
- Passive-aggressive hedging: "it is difficult to understand why..."
- Victim framing: "despite our best efforts..."

ADVERSARIAL TONE INDICATORS (score → 0.30):
- Direct challenge to panel competence
- Accusations of process unfairness
- Implied corruption or bias
- Comparative diminishment: "unlike peer institutions which were treated differently..."

EMOTIONAL TONE INDICATORS (score → 0.20):
- Appeals to institutional loyalty or gratitude
- Impact narratives about students/staff suffering
- Urgency/desperation language
- Superlatives without evidence: "extremely dedicated", "significantly impacted"
```

### Banned Words & Replacement Strategy

The following words and phrases trigger institutional defensive bias. Their use is prohibited in all Acuterium outputs under PSI-DOMINION governance.

**Full Banned Lexicon:**

| Banned Word/Phrase | Why Banned | Approved Replacement |
|---|---|---|
| unfair | Implies institutional injustice — activates defensive consolidation | "procedural inconsistency" or "alignment gap with [rule X]" |
| biased | Direct attack on panel integrity — maximum PJS activation | "divergent interpretation of [criterion Y]" |
| wrong | Bald accusation — challenges authority | "the calculation, when re-executed per [formula], yields [result]" |
| error | Implies incompetence | "discrepancy between [output] and [standard]" |
| mistake | Same as error | "variance from the prescribed methodology" |
| trap | Conspiratorial framing | Remove entirely; reframe as "procedural ambiguity in the timeline" |
| failure | Assigns blame | "gap identified for development" or "area for alignment" |
| incompetent | Career-destroying accusation | Remove entirely |
| negligent | Legal accusation | Remove entirely; route to legal claim if applicable |
| ignored | Implies deliberate disregard | "not addressed in the panel's published rationale" |
| flawed | Broad attack | "specific variance from [standard X] at [section Y]" |
| arbitrary | Attacks legitimacy | "not explicitly mapped to a published criterion" |
| violated | Accusatory | "diverges from the prescribed process" |
| discriminatory | High-risk legal allegation | "inconsistent with peer institution treatment documented in [record]" |
| absurd | Contemptuous | Remove entirely |
| clearly wrong | Dismissive | Replace with specific factual restatement |
| demanded | Aggressive verb | "requested" or "required by [rule X]" |
| forced | Coercion framing | "required by the applicable standard" |
| refused | Adversarial verb | "did not" or "the panel's response did not address" |
| attack | Conflict framing | "adversarial challenge" — only in legal contexts |
| victimized | Emotional | Remove entirely |

**Replacement Strategy — Three Rules:**

1. **Reframe as process:** Replace blame language with process-neutral description. "The panel was wrong" → "The calculation formula produces [result] when applied to [data]."
2. **Reframe as alignment:** Replace deficit language with gap/alignment language. "The institution failed" → "The institution has identified [area] for alignment with [standard]."
3. **Anchor to rule text:** Replace opinion with citation. "This is unfair" → "Section 4.2(b) requires X. The [document] demonstrates X was satisfied on [date]."

### Persuasion Techniques — PSI-DOMINION Methodology

**Technique 1: Silent Authority Doctrine**
Never demand correction — enable the institution to correct itself in the interest of its own integrity.
```
IMPLEMENTATION:
"The Authority will wish to confirm that its own published formula, when applied to the 
documented data, produces the result set out in Appendix A. The institution respectfully 
makes this calculation available to assist the review."
```

**Technique 2: Hybrid Trust Persuasion**
Combine objective evidence with institutional value alignment.
```
STRUCTURE:
[Evidence] + [Rule text] + [Institutional interest in alignment]
Example: "The board minutes of [date] confirm [action]. ISAM v2 Section 1.6.3 requires 
this action. Confirming compliance with this requirement strengthens the accreditation 
record's integrity."
```

**Technique 3: Active Verification Questions**
Convert assertions to answerable binary questions. Questions are psychologically harder to dismiss than assertions.
```
CONVERSION:
Assertion: "The institution meets Standard C1.6."
Question: "Does the strategic plan approved by the board on [date], as documented in 
Exhibit A, satisfy the requirement in ISAM v2 C1.6(vi)? The institution submits that 
it does, for the following reasons."
```

**Technique 4: Cognitive Load Discipline**
Structure arguments so the panel can confirm — not construct — the logic.
```
TEMPLATE:
"[Rule X] requires [Y].
The institution [action] on [date], documented at [Exhibit N].
This action satisfies [Y] because [one-sentence logical bridge].
The panel is invited to verify this conclusion against the criterion text."
```

**Technique 5: Non-Threat Posture**
Never position the appeal as a challenge to authority. Frame it as assistance.
```
LANGUAGE PATTERN:
WRONG: "We are challenging the panel's finding on C1.6."
RIGHT: "The institution provides the following information to assist the panel in 
completing its review of criterion C1.6."
```

**Technique 6: Outcome-First Structure**
State the remedy or conclusion in the first sentence of each section, then justify.
```
TEMPLATE:
"Criterion C1.6 is satisfied. [Then provide 3-5 supporting points.]"
vs.
"The institution has a strategic plan which was approved by..." [panel has to read to find the conclusion]
```


### Step 6 — Document Reconstruction Recommendations

For flagged sections:
1. Provide specific banned-word replacements
2. Provide tone rewrite using Technique 1-6 templates
3. Provide structure upgrade recommendation
4. Compute projected η_i after revision

## Guardrails

**NEVER DO:**
1. Never approve a document containing banned words — replacements are mandatory
2. Never score η_i above 0.80 for a section using defensive or adversarial tone
3. Never compromise on Gate 6 compliance — η ≥ 0.80 is non-negotiable before release

**ALWAYS DO:**

3. Always provide specific sentence-level rewrite instructions, not abstract guidance
4. Always project the post-revision η_i to confirm the revision will achieve Gate 6 compliance
5. Always calibrate to the specific audience type — a tone that works for regulators fails for executives
